Scaling Ethereum with Rollups

Ethereum is the world's decentralized computer, but high usage leads to congestion and expensive gas fees. Layer 2 networks like Arbitrum process transactions off-chain, rollup the results into a single package, and post it to Ethereum—retaining L1's security at a fraction of the cost.

Scaling Under the Hood

Understanding the technology that drives Arbitrum and Ethereum L2 scaling.

⛓️

Why Ethereum Needed L2

Ethereum processes roughly 15 transactions per second (TPS). When demand rises, users bid up transaction fees (gas) to secure quick execution, occasionally pushing a simple swap past $50. Layer 2 networks process trades off-chain to ease this load.

📦

The Role of Arbitrum

Arbitrum operates as an Optimistic Rollup. It assumes transactions are valid ("optimistic") and aggregates hundreds of L2 operations into a compressed package, submitting the state roots to Ethereum L1. This yields high throughput and low costs.

🛡️

L1-Grade Security

Unlike private chains or sidechains, Arbitrum publishes its data on Ethereum. If a sequencer tries to post fraudulent data, validator nodes can challenge it during the "Fraud Proof Window." The dispute is settled on Ethereum L1, keeping L2 secure.
